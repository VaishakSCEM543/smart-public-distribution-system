# Problem Statement – Smart Public Distribution System

India’s Public Distribution System (PDS) is one of the largest food security networks in the world, serving over 800 million beneficiaries. Despite digitization efforts, significant inefficiencies persist at the point of ration delivery.

## Key Issues

- Leakage and diversion of subsidized food grains  
- Manual weighing and human dependency  
- Inaccurate quantity distribution  
- Lack of physical verification during dispensing  
- Limited real-time monitoring  
- Long queues and waiting times  

Most current systems digitize records but do not control the **physical dispensing process**, which allows manipulation at fair price shops.

## Core Problem

There is no automated, sensor-verified dispensing mechanism that ensures beneficiaries receive the exact entitled quantity while simultaneously logging transactions securely in real time.
